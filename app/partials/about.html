erica vhay. her story.

Erica has always pursued art. It is in her heart, her head and her history.

  • 1970

    Foundation Carved in Stone

    Erica Vhay was born into a family of architects and artists. Her great grandfather Gutzon Borglum and his son Lincoln Borglum - were famous for their sculpting of Mt. Rushmore and many other works of art.

  • 1993

    Art in the Heart

    Erica received a degree in Ceramics from the University of Oregon. She spent much of her education in art classes, emphasizing ceramics, sculpture and drawing. She opened her own studio after graduating in 1993, producing sculpture and functional pottery for two years in Eugene.

  • 1999

    Designer Looking for More...

    Erica moved to Boulder, CO and took up graphic design, working on industrial graphic design for music CDs and product packaging. While living in Boulder, she married a great guy who had followed her from Eugene. It was also during this time she took up drawing in earnest - working primarily with pastels. In 1999 she moved to a remote ranch in Montana, an elk hoof's throw from the Yellowstone river. She continued sketching and began painting - taking classes at Montana State University.

  • 2001

    New Faces

    From there she moved to the Phoenix area and produced two fine works that are still in progress - Miles Lincoln Brown, born 2001, and Leopold Ivan Brown, born 2003. Though the sculpting of these two works impacted her productivity for several years, she excelled as a web designer.

  • 2006

    Getting Settled -- Getting Started

    Erica and family moved to Flagstaff, AZ in 2006. Erica focused on oil painting from this time forward. Her subject matter is in constant rotation - with frequent portraits, landscapes, and foods. The scale of her paintings is also highly variable - ranging from 6"x6" to 60"x60". She currently shows work at several venues.
