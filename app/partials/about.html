her story.

talent. dedication. courage.

Erica's talent is evident in her compositions and ability to convey emotional content in her work. Professional artistic execution requires more than talent; it requires long hours of practice. Erica's dedication to her work has kept her in the studio day after day for years to develop her skill. Her talent and dedication have served her well. In addition to these ingredients, Erica's creative secret is courage. Courage gives her ability to continue experimenting, to apply her brush boldly, and evaluate her work unflinchingly.

Her subject matter varies with frequent forays into portraiture, landscapes, food, and abstracts. The scale of her paintings varies widely as well - ranging from small 6" x 6" pieces to huge 60" x 60".

Today, she shows in several venues. If you are interested in adding Erica's work to your venue, please contact Darryl Brown.

1970
Ancestry

Erica Vhay was born into a family of architects and artists. Her great grandfather Gutzon Borglum and his son Lincoln Borglum were famous for their sculpting of Mt. Rushmore and many other renowned works of art.

1993
Education

Erica received a degree in Art History from the University of Oregon. She spent much of her education in art classes, emphasizing ceramics, sculpture and drawing. She opened her own ceramics studio after graduating in 1993, producing sculpture and functional pottery.

1999
Profession

Erica moved to Boulder, CO and worked as a graphic designer, creating industrial graphic designs for audio CDs and product packaging. While living in Boulder, she married a great guy who had followed her from Eugene. It was also during this time changed her artistic focus from ceramics to drawing ‐ working primarily with pastels. In 1999 she moved to a remote ranch in Montana, an elk hoof's throw from the Yellowstone river. She continued sketching and painting - taking classes at Montana State University.

2001
Progeny

From there she moved to the Phoenix area and produced two fine works that are still in progress - Miles Lincoln Brown, born 2001, and Leopold Ivan Brown, born 2003. Though the sculpting of these two works impacted her productivity in other areas for a time, she excelled as a web designer and continued producing her own visual artwork.

2006
Focus

Erica and family moved to Flagstaff, AZ in 2006. Erica focused on oil painting from this point forward. She began producing focused work and was able to dedicate a big part of her life to her art. She began showing at many venues in Flagstaff, Arizona and selling her unique style of work. Her portraiture in particular was a new take on a classic style and has been well recieved.

2016
Today

Over the last ten years, Erica's career has picked up wide following with collectors across the United States. She has participated in many shows as an artist and juried shows as well.
